### [The safety failures we are not instrumenting: a perspective on hidden safety-critical challenges in modern AI systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19292v1)
**Authors:** Gjergji Kasneci, Enkelejda Kasneci
**Published:** 2026-07-21
**Summary:** AI safety must expand from visible failures to hidden socio-technical risks across five integrity layers.

**What's new**
- Proposes a five-layer framework: epistemic, control, temporal, organizational, and ecosystem integrity.
- Identifies under-recognized risks like overreliance, reward hacking, memory poisoning, and model collapse.
- Argues that many failures are quiet, distributed, and normalized before being recognized as hazards.
- Calls for shifting AI safety from model-centric evaluation to socio-technical reliability.

### [Copy Less, Ground More: Overcoming Repetitive Copying in Long-Context Reasoning via Evidence-Aware Reinforcement Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19345v1)
**Authors:** Lizhe Fang, Weizhou Shen, Tianyi Tang, Yisen Wang
**Published:** 2026-07-21
**Summary:** GEAR uses evidence-aware rewards to reduce repetitive copying in long-context LLM reasoning, improving accuracy by up to +4.6 points.

**What's new**
- Identifies 'repetitive copying' as a failure mode in long-context LLMs, worsening with context length.
- Shows root cause is insufficient grounding: models copy indiscriminately, missing key evidence.
- Proposes GEAR, a reward shaping method with grounding reward and distractor penalty.
- Develops automated pipeline to create evidence-annotated training data from arbitrary documents.
- Achieves consistent accuracy gains, especially in longer contexts, while reducing copying and thinking length.

### [ATLAS: A Foundation Neural Sampler for Amorphous Materials](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19198v1)
**Authors:** Mouyang Cheng, Denis Blessing, Botao Yu, Gerhard Neumann, Mingda Li, Carles Domingo-Enrich, Yuanqi Du
**Published:** 2026-07-21
**Summary:** ATLAS is a diffusion-based neural sampler that efficiently generates Boltzmann-distributed amorphous structures, enabling accurate thermodynamics and inverse design.

**What's new**
- Learns a diffusion process to sample amorphous structures directly from a target energy function.
- Generalizes across system size, temperature, and composition via an equivariant graph neural network.
- Achieves <0.2% free energy error in glass regime with >500x fewer energy evaluations than parallel tempering.
- Steers structures toward target order parameters and optimized bulk moduli in metallic glasses.
- Composition-amortized pretraining reduces inverse-design costs by several hundred-fold.

### [Thermodynamics-Informed Input Reparameterization for Neural Prediction of Real-Fluid Thermodynamic Properties in Supercritical Combustion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19241v1)
**Authors:** Haoze Zhang, Han Li, Ke Xiao, Yangchen Xu, Runze Mao, Zhi X. Chen
**Published:** 2026-07-21
**Summary:** A thermodynamics-informed input reparameterization (TAIR) improves neural network prediction of real-fluid properties in supercritical combustion.

**What's new**
- TAIR replaces raw enthalpy with target-aligned thermodynamic coordinates for each property network.
- Temperature network uses an ideal-gas enthalpy inversion; density/compressibility networks use ideal-gas density estimates.
- Networks learn real-fluid departures from ideal-gas baselines instead of full closure from raw enthalpy.
- TAIR reduces RMSE by factors of 1.5–7.5 for T, ρ, ψ; up to 14.5 for unseen strain-rate flames.

### [Complete Hierarchy of Nonrelativistic Odd-Parity Spin Splitting in Collinear Magnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19303v1)
**Authors:** Yichen Liu, Junxi Yu, Pu Zhang, Cheng-Cheng Liu
**Published:** 2026-07-21
**Summary:** Complete classification of odd-parity spin splitting in collinear magnets, revealing h-, k-, and m-wave forms beyond p- and f-wave.

**What's new**
- Identified h- (ℓ=5), k- (ℓ=7), and m-wave (ℓ=9) odd-parity spin splittings in collinear magnets.
- Derived full mapping from crystallographic irreps to lowest-order odd-parity basis functions.
- Constructed minimal lattice models realizing h-, k-, and m-wave splittings.
- Predicted light-driven h- and k-wave phases in Fe₂TeO₆ and MgFe₆Ge₆ with observable spin splittings.
- Showed h- and k-wave allow anomalous Hall effect, while m-wave forbids it.

### [One geometric barrier unifies melting, vitrification and jamming of hard spheres in all dimensions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19185v1)
**Authors:** Sujin B Babu
**Published:** 2026-07-21
**Summary:** A unified geometric theory explains melting, vitrification, and jamming of hard spheres across dimensions using three exact ingredients.

**What's new**
- Derives Lindemann criterion from contact theorem, isotropy identity, and first-passage argument.
- Predicts kinetic glass transition, random close packing, Kauzmann point, and crystal melting in d=3-12.
- All five transitions lie on a single barrier surface, matching simulations and replica theory within a few percent.
- Predicts Lindemann constant 0.13 in 3D, decreasing with dimension; verified in 2D with specific arrest and jamming densities.
