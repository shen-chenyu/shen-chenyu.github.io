### [UniClawBench: A Universal Benchmark for Proactive Agents on Real-World Tasks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08768v1)

**Published:** 2026-07-09
**Summary:** UniClawBench is a capability-driven benchmark for evaluating proactive agents in dynamic, real-world settings using live Docker containers.

**What's new**
- First benchmark focused on five foundational capabilities: Skill Usage, Exploration, Long-Context Reasoning, Multimodal Understanding, Cross-Platform Coordination.

### [Score Accuracy Along the Forward Diffusion Does Not Certify Numerical Stability in Diffusion Sampling](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08757v1)
**Authors:** Yiwei Zhou
**Published:** 2026-07-09
**Summary:** Small score matching error under forward diffusion does not guarantee stable reverse sampling; Euler-Maruyama can diverge in Wasserstein distance.

**What's new**
- Constructs smooth score field with tiny forward-marginal error but divergent Euler-Maruyama moments.
- Shows weak convergence can hold while all Wasserstein distances diverge.
- Same failure occurs within fixed finite neural architecture with bounded Lipschitz denoisers.
- Positive result: projecting denoiser onto compact support yields Wasserstein convergence.
- Experiments with DiT-style network confirm rare trajectory growth suppressed by projection.

### [Super Weights in LLMs and the Failure of Selective Training](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08733v1)
**Authors:** Shreyas Subramanian, Adewale Akinfaderin, Akarsha Sehwag
**Published:** 2026-07-09
**Summary:** Super Weights are not universally critical; training them in isolation fails, while structured methods like LoRA succeed.

**What's new**
- Pruning Super Weights degrades some LLMs but not all, contradicting prior claims.
- Isolated training of Super Weights (up to 8K params) drops accuracy to random guessing.
- Expanding to local neighborhoods (36K params) does not improve performance.
- Randomly chosen positions in the same layers improve over baseline, showing failure is specific to Super Weights.
- LoRA with 0.16% parameters succeeds, but constraining it to Super Weight coordinates fails.

### [Exciton valley depolarization in monolayer MoS2: non-Markovian quantum dynamics, intervalley scattering, and the breakdown of the Dyakonov-Perel mechanism](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08479v1)

**Published:** 2026-07-09
**Summary:** Exciton valley depolarization in monolayer MoS2 is dominated by intervalley scattering, not the Dyakonov-Perel mechanism.

**What's new**
- Realistic exciton-phonon scattering is too weak to induce motional narrowing in the DP regime.
- Large momentum intervalley scattering shortens depolarization by 3-4x vs intravalley-only models.
- Valley depolarization time is ~50 fs at 300 K and ~130 fs at 10 K.
- Markovian Lindblad framework artificially amplifies scattering, biasing toward DP interpretation.
- NEGF+GKBA approach treats exchange and scattering on equal footing across full Brillouin zone.

### [Quantum weight and low-loss EELS signatures of Wannier quantum geometry in black phosphorus](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08438v1)

**Published:** 2026-07-09
**Summary:** Quantum geometry in black phosphorus is probed via low-loss EELS, revealing nearly isotropic quantum weight despite strong band-mass anisotropy.

**What's new**
- First-principles framework connects momentum-resolved quantum metric to direction-resolved quantum weight measurable via low-loss EELS.
- Single-band quantum metric of top valence band requires masked maps and projectors due to folding degeneracies and near degeneracies.

- Orbital-resolved Hubbard-Hartree corrections cause sub-percent rigid shifts and a small armchair-directed drift of Kzz/Kxx.
