### [RoboTTT: Context Scaling for Robot Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15275v1)
**Authors:** Yunfan Jiang, Yevgen Chebotar, Ruijie Zheng, Fengyuan Hu, Yunhao Ge, Jimmy Wu, Tianyuan Dai, Scott Reed, Li Fei-Fei, Yuke Zhu, Linxi "Jim" Fan
**Published:** 2026-07-16
**Summary:** RoboTTT scales robot policy context to 8K timesteps via test-time training, enabling one-shot imitation and long-horizon tasks.

**What's new**
- Context length scaled to 8K timesteps, 1000x beyond prior policies, without latency increase.
- Enables one-shot in-context imitation from human video and on-the-fly policy improvement.
- First demonstration of steady performance gains as pretraining context length increases.
- Integrates test-time training into robot foundation models via fast-weight recurrent states.
- Achieves 87% improvement over single-step baselines and completes 10-stage assembly tasks.

### [MemPoison: Uncovering Persistent Memory Threats and Structural Blind Spots in LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14651v1)
**Authors:** Jifeng Gao, Kang Xia, Yi Zhang, Xiaobin Hong, Mingkai Lin, Xingshen Wei, Wenzhong Li, Sanglu Lu
**Published:** 2026-07-16
**Summary:** MemPoison reveals persistent memory vulnerabilities in LLM agents, showing that adversarial content can be injected via standard channels and evade write-time defenses.

**What's new**
- Introduces MemPoison, a benchmark with 1227 cases across four attack types and three injection channels.
- Proposes a three-tier taxonomy: direct single-record, compositional multi-record, and context-triggered dormant corruption.
- Shows write-time defenses fail against compositional and dormant attacks despite suppressing direct ones.
- Uses mechanistic influence decomposition to expose structural blind spots in static filtering defenses.

### [BadWAM: When World-Action Models Dream Right but Act Wrong](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15207v1)
**Authors:** Qi Li, Xingyi Yang, Xinchao Wang
**Published:** 2026-07-16
**Summary:** BadWAM reveals that world-action models can be fooled by small visual perturbations, causing them to imagine correctly but act wrongly.

**What's new**
- Introduces World-Action Drift Attacks, a new class of adversarial attacks targeting WAMs.
- Defines two attack types: action-only (disruptive) and imagination-preserving (stealthy).
- Shows action-only attack reduces task success from 96.5% to 43.1%.
- Demonstrates that WAMs can imagine a plausible future while executing harmful actions.

### [Emergent d-wave altermagnetism in chlorine-adsorbed FeSe monolayer](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15197v1)
**Authors:** Zi-Hao Ding, Ze-Feng Gao, Kai Liu, Peng-Jie Guo, Zhong-Yi Lu
**Published:** 2026-07-16
**Summary:** Proposes chlorine-adsorbed FeSe monolayer as a robust altermagnet with giant spin splitting, coexisting with superconductivity.

**What's new**

### [High-Q superconducting microwave resonators using MBE titanium nitride](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15230v1)

### [Coulomb blockade in microscopic material defects as a source of decoherence and noise in solid-state quantum circuits](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15252v1)
**Authors:** R. Banerjee, L. P. Lindoy, M. Hegedus, A. Hutcheson, T. Hawkins, E. Daghigh-Ahmadi, S. Samaddar, T. Barker, J. P. Goff, A. Ya. Tzalenchuk, I. Rungger, S. E. de Graaf
**Published:** 2026-07-16
**Summary:** Metallic grains in superconducting circuits cause decoherence via Coulomb blockade, challenging the TLS defect paradigm.

**What's new**
- Identified Coulomb blockade in metallic grains as a new decoherence source in quantum circuits.
- Scanning gate microscopy revealed microwave-driven charge tunneling in ubiquitous grains.
- These defects are as common as TLS defects but arise from a different physical mechanism.

- Eliminating metallic grains during fabrication offers a clear path to improved coherence.

### [Bridge Evidence: Static Retrieval Utility Does Not Predict Causal Utility in Multi-Step Agentic Search](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15253v1)
**Authors:** Debayan Mukhopadhyay, Utshab Kumar Ghosh, Shubham Chatterjee
**Published:** 2026-07-16
**Summary:** Static retrieval utility does not predict causal utility in multi-step agentic search; bridge documents are causally important but look useless to static readers.

**What's new**
- Counterfactual Trajectory Utility (CTU) measures causal document impact by deleting it and re-running the agent's trajectory.
- Static RAG Utility (SRU) and CTU are nearly independent (Spearman rho = -0.026) across 23,322 observations.
- About a third of documents are 'bridge documents'—causally load-bearing but useless to static readers.
- Bridge documents provide discriminative entities that redirect the agent's next query (4.02x more often).
- Optimizing static relevance does not deliver causal usefulness in agentic retrieval.
