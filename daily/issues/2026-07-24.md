### [PG-KINN: A Physics-Informed Petrov-Galerkin Kolmogorov-Arnold Network for Solving Forward and Inverse PDEs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20378v1)
**Authors:** Amirhossein Sadr, Nima Soltani, Vahideh Moghtadaiee, Aida Pakniyat, Dara Rahmati, Saeid Gorgin
**Published:** 2026-07-22
**Summary:** PG-KINN uses a Petrov-Galerkin formulation with KAN trial and polynomial test spaces to solve forward and inverse PDEs more accurately than MLP or KAN baselines.

**What's new**
- Replaces strong-form residual minimization with a Petrov-Galerkin weak form, reducing derivative order and improving conditioning.
- Uses independent piecewise-polynomial test spaces with Gauss-Legendre quadrature, enabling handling of non-self-adjoint and inverse problems.
- Outperforms MLP and state-of-the-art KAN methods on benchmarks including crack singularities, hyperelasticity, and heterogeneous media.

### [Interval and fuzzy physics-augmented neural networks (iPANN and fPANN) for uncertainty quantification and propagation in constitutive modeling](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20339v1)
**Authors:** Somesh Pratap Singh, Govinda Anantha Padmanabha, Jingye Tan, Steven Yang, Reese E. Jones, D. Thomas Seidl, Nikolaos Bouklas
**Published:** 2026-07-22
**Summary:** iPANN and fPANN are physics-augmented neural networks that quantify and propagate uncertainty in hyperelastic constitutive modeling using interval and fuzzy set representations.

**What's new**
- iPANNs learn sparse lower, mean, and upper free energy branches to enclose noisy stress observations.
- fPANNs extend iPANNs with fuzzy-set alpha-cut interpolation for nested admissible responses.
- Mechanistic constraints (objectivity, consistency, polyconvexity) and smoothed L0 regularization ensure interpretability.
- A two-stage transfer-learning procedure trains the bound models efficiently.
- Uncertainty propagation is demonstrated in finite element simulations.

### [Quantum Kernels and the Cross-Section of Stock Returns: Anatomy of a Vanishing Advantage](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20168v1)
**Authors:** Junchi Shen
**Published:** 2026-07-22
**Summary:** Quantum kernels show no advantage over classical RBF in stock return prediction after controlling for data leakage and budget.

**What's new**
- Controlled horse race on Chinese A-shares (2012-2025) finds no quantum advantage over RBF kernel.
- Quantum kernels match but never beat equal-budget linear models after family-wise correction.
- Apparent quantum advantage arises from data leakage: full-sample screening and fewer walk-forward windows.
- Geometric difference (g>1) does not predict out-of-sample gains (ρ=-0.20).
- Proposes protocol standards for valid quantum advantage claims in finance.

### [Statevector-Referenced Geometry Survival of a Four-Qubit ZZ Quantum Kernel on IBM Quantum Hardware: A Fixed-Subset Diagnostic Across Three Execution Configurations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20377v1)
**Authors:** Rostyslav Sipakov
**Published:** 2026-07-22
**Summary:** A study measuring how well a four-qubit quantum kernel preserves its intended geometry on IBM hardware, finding gate twirling most faithful but fidelity and task relevance are distinct.

**What's new**
- Gate twirling alone was most faithful across all geometry axes, with jackknife-resolved improvement over baseline.
- Dynamical decoupling alone showed no significant improvement over baseline at the frozen-window scale.
- Residual hardware distortion, not finite sampling, dominates the discrepancy between intended and executed geometry.
- Fidelity and label alignment were reversed: the most faithful configuration had the lowest centered kernel-target alignment.
