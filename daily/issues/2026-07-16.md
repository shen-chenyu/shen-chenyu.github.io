Today's papers span AI agent efficiency, fundamental limits of video diffusion, and new materials physics. ## Featured Papers

### [Do AI Agents Know When a Task Is Simple? Toward Complexity-Aware Reasoning and Execution](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13034v1)
**Authors:** Junjie Yin, Xinyu Feng
**Published:** 2026-07-14
**Summary:** LLM agents waste resources by over-reading; E3 cuts cost 85% by estimating task difficulty before executing.

**What's new**
- Formalizes minimum-sufficient execution and Agent Cognitive Redundancy Ratio (ACRR).
- Proposes E3 (Estimate, Execute, Expand) to avoid unnecessary context re-reading.
- On MSE-Bench, E3 matches 100% success while cutting cost 85%, tokens 91%, files 92%.
- Beats strong adaptive retrieval baseline by 16% and generalizes to held-out instructions.
- Real-model test on gpt-4o shows E3 is leanest and fastest with comparable task success.

### [The Seriality Gap in Video Diffusion Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13031v1)
**Authors:** Jorge Diaz Chao, Konpat Preechakul, Yuxi Liu, Yutong Bai
**Published:** 2026-07-14
**Summary:** Video diffusion models struggle with tasks requiring serial reasoning, termed the seriality gap.

**What's new**
- Performance degrades as causal chains lengthen in multi-ball dynamics.
- Single-ball control shows degradation is due to dependent events, not video length.
- Autoregressive generation and deeper architectures improve performance.
- Denoising steps do not add serial computation beyond the backbone.

### [TerraZero: Procedural Driving Simulation for Zero-Demonstration Self-Play at Scale](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13028v1)
**Authors:** Zhouchonghao Wu, Akshay Rangesh, Weixin Li, Wei-Jer Chang, Zachary Lee, Tim Wang, Wei Zhan
**Published:** 2026-07-14
**Summary:** TerraZero is a procedural driving simulator enabling zero-demonstration self-play training of robust autonomous driving policies at scale.

**What's new**
- Configurable C engine with zero-copy CPU/GPU path sustains 1.3M agent-steps/sec on a single GPU.
- Randomized rule-based road users, dynamics, rewards, and sizes per episode yield unbounded scenarios from logged maps.
- First fully learned policy to top InterPlan long-tail benchmark, ahead of larger learned planners.
- Policies generalize zero-shot across cities and datasets, including emergent left-hand-traffic driving.
- Same stack serves both ego driving policies and sim agents controlling vehicles, pedestrians, and cyclists.

**Published:** 2026-07-14

**What's new**
- Runs natively on mobile phones, managing sessions, memory, skills, tools, and agent loop directly on the device.

### [A Shortcut to Statistically Steady-State Turbulence with Flow Matching](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13022v1)
**Authors:** Gianluca Galletti, Gerald Gutenbrunner, William Hornsby, Lorenzo Zanisi, Naomi Carey, Stanislas Pamela, Johannes Brandstetter, Fabian Paischer
**Published:** 2026-07-14
**Summary:** GyroFlow uses flow matching to directly generate statistically steady-state turbulence snapshots, bypassing costly transient simulations.

**What's new**
- Bypasses explicit time evolution by modeling saturated state distribution under ergodicity.
- Generates 5D gyrokinetic turbulence snapshots from noise conditioned on operating parameters.
- Outperforms autoregressive, reduced-order, and other generative models with substantial speedup.
- Introduces FGyD metric for evaluating generation quality correlated with downstream accuracy.
- Can warm-start numerical solvers to accelerate convergence to steady state.

### [Audio-Native Speech Recognition with a Frozen Discrete-Diffusion Language Model](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13013v1)
**Authors:** Harsha Vardhan Khurdula, Abhinav Kumar Singh, Yoeven D Khemlani, Vineet Agarwal
**Published:** 2026-07-14
**Summary:** A frozen discrete-diffusion LM (DiffusionGemma) transcribes speech in parallel via an audio-native interface, achieving 6.6% WER on LibriSpeech.

**What's new**
- First use of a discrete diffusion LM (not autoregressive) for ASR, refining whole transcripts in parallel.
- Frozen Whisper encoder + lightweight projector + low-rank adapters; only 0.16% of backbone parameters trained.
- CTC loss through frozen output head solves gradient deadlock from standard diffusion objectives.
- Transcribes in ~8 parallel steps regardless of utterance length; single adapter trained on 6 languages.
