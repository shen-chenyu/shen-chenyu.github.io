### [Requential Coding: Pushing the Limits of Model Compression with Self-Generated Training Data](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11883v1)
**Authors:** Shikai Qiu, Marc Finzi, Yujia Zheng, Kun Zhang, Andrew Gordon Wilson
**Published:** 2026-07-13
**Summary:** Requential coding compresses models by having a teacher select training samples from the student's distribution, yielding code lengths independent of parameter count and data entropy.

**What's new**
- Code length is independent of parameter count and data entropy, often orders of magnitude shorter than prequential coding.
- Larger models and ensembles compress to much smaller sizes despite more parameters, holding loss fixed.
- Plugged into PAC-Bayes bound, yields state-of-the-art generalization guarantees for billion-parameter LLMs.
- Predicts gradual overfitting when models are trained for multiple epochs.
- Isolates learnable information from random content, revealing lower-entropy text has more learnable structure than images.

**Published:** 2026-07-13
**Summary:** Direct thermal scanning probe writing enables site-selective synthesis of individual perovskite quantum dots with <25 nm precision and high single-photon purity.

**What's new**
- Demonstrated direct writing of perovskite quantum dots with individual-emitter resolution using a thermal scanning probe method.

### [Strain-controlled crystalline--amorphous transition and flat-band tuning in buckled silicon kagome](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11790v1)
**Authors:** Chenhaoyue Wang, Amartya S. Banerjee
**Published:** 2026-07-13
**Summary:** Buckled silicon kagome lattice enables strain-controlled flat-band tuning and crystalline–amorphous transition.

**What's new**
- Proposes buckled silicon kagome lattice (SiKL) with a flat band near the Fermi level.
- Biaxial tension at 10% strain narrows bandwidth and hardens soft phonons.
- MD simulations show strain-controlled crystalline–amorphous transition near 2% strain.
- Higher strains cause abrupt, first-order-like collapse with transition temperature up to 600 K.
- Ag(111) substrate may supply epitaxial strain to preserve flat band and order above room temperature.
