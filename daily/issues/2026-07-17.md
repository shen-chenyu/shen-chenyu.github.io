**Authors:** Zexun Wang
**Published:** 2026-07-15
**Summary:** CAVA converts heterogeneous agent runtime records into canonical action objects for verifiable governance.

**What's new**
- Formalizes canonical action identity and semantic pattern detection for heterogeneous agent runtimes.

- Provides runtime-portable projection and optional attestation substrates for independent verification.

### [Dirac topology, anomalous Hall response, and giant magnetoresistance in carrier-compensated altermagnetic semimetal NiS](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13400v1)
**Authors:** Shovan Gayen, Sk. Soyeb Ali, S K Panda
**Published:** 2026-07-15
**Summary:** First-principles study identifies hexagonal NiS as a 3d altermagnetic semimetal with intertwined topology, magnetism, and giant magnetoresistance.

**What's new**
- NiS exhibits momentum-dependent spin splitting from rotational coset symmetry of the NiAs lattice.
- Gapped Dirac crossings produce intense Berry-curvature hot spots and nearly compensated electron-hole pockets.
- Large intrinsic spin Hall conductivity comparable to 4d/5d metals and anomalous Hall response at zero net magnetization.
- Nonsaturating magnetoresistance exceeds 10000% due to carrier compensation.
- Exchange tensor from first principles reveals long-range superexchange, reproducing experimental Neel temperature.

### [Hybridization-controlled re-entrant electronic phase switching and moire-confined states in twisted bilayer PtTe2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13529v1)

**Published:** 2026-07-15
**Summary:** Twisted bilayer PtTe2 shows re-entrant electronic gap switching controlled by interlayer hybridization and moiré confinement.

**What's new**
- Non-monotonic gap evolution: gapless at 7.34° and 60°, gapped at intermediate angles.
