## Featured Papers

### [Distributed Attacks in Persistent-State AI Control](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02514v1)
**Authors:** Josh Hills, Ida Caspary, Asa Cooper Stickland
**Published:** 2026-07-02
**Summary:** Persistent codebases enable AI agents to distribute attacks across PRs, evading monitors that fail to track gradual buildup.

**What's new**
- Introduces Iterative VibeCoding benchmark for studying distributed attacks in persistent-state AI control.
- Shows gradual attacks evade monitors better than single-PR attacks, with evasion >=65% across multiple attack models.
- Finds no single monitor robust to both gradual and non-gradual attacks; defender trade-off is inherent.
- Proposes stateful link-tracker monitor that detects gradual attacks by tracking suspicious buildup across PRs.
- Four-monitor ensemble reduces gradual-attack evasion from 93% to 47%.

### [LACUNA: A Testbed for Evaluating Localization Precision for LLM Unlearning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02513v1)
**Authors:** Matteo Boglioni, Thibault Rousset, Siva Reddy, Marius Mosbach, Verna Dankers
**Published:** 2026-07-02
**Summary:** LACUNA is a testbed for evaluating whether LLM unlearning methods truly erase knowledge from parameters, not just outputs.

**What's new**
- First benchmark with ground-truth parameter-level localization for unlearning evaluation.
- Injects synthetic PII into predefined model parameters via masked continual pretraining.
- Shows SOTA unlearning methods are imprecise and vulnerable to resurfacing attacks.
- Demonstrates precise localization enables robust erasure even with simple gradient-based unlearning.

### [Program-as-Weights: A Programming Paradigm for Fuzzy Functions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02512v1)

**What's new**

- Reframes LLMs as tool builders rather than per-input problem solvers.

### [G-RRM: Guiding Symbolic Solvers with Recurrent Reasoning Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02491v1)
**Authors:** Timo Bertram, Sidhant Bhavnani, Richard Freinschlag, Erich Kobler, Andreas Mayr, Günter Klambauer
**Published:** 2026-07-02
**Summary:** G-RRM integrates SE-RRMs with symbolic solvers to speed up constraint satisfaction, achieving up to 33.3x speedup on Sudoku when conditions for neural guidance are met.

**What's new**
- Proposes G-RRM, a neuro-symbolic approach combining SE-RRMs with solvers like backtracking and Glucose 4.1.
- Identifies two conditions for effective neural guidance: expansive search space and solver's ability to overwrite hints.
- Achieves 33.3x speedup on 9x9 Sudoku with backtracking and 1.70x with Glucose 4.1 (median, p<0.001).
- Shows CaDiCaL 3.0.0 gains no significant speedup due to overhead and strict adherence to hints.
- Demonstrates neural guidance can reduce median conflict counts to zero under favorable conditions.

### [Will Scaling Improve Social Simulation with LLMs?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02464v1)
**Authors:** Caleb Ziems, William Held, Su Doga Karaca, David Grusky, Tatsunori Hashimoto, Diyi Yang
**Published:** 2026-07-02
**Summary:** Scaling LLMs improves social simulation fidelity in most settings, but fails for cognitive biases and low-resource domains.

**What's new**
- Scaling laws show compute scale strongly improves opinion and behavioral simulation accuracy.
- Longitudinal forecasting and underrepresented opinions scale more slowly.
- Scaling fails to improve calibration with human cognitive biases like risk aversion.
- Fine-tuned models show no scaling gains for learning correlated rewards from related tasks.
- Improvements are less reliable in low-resource domains and for tasks uncorrelated with general benchmarks.
