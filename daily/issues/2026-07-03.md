### [Is One Layer Enough? Training A Single Transformer Layer Can Match Full-Parameter RL Training](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01232v1)
**Authors:** Zijian Zhang, Rizhen Hu, Athanasios Glentis, Dawei Li, Chung-Yiu Yau, Hongzhou Lin, Mingyi Hong
**Published:** 2026-07-01
**Summary:** Training a single transformer layer can match or exceed full-parameter RL gains, with high-contribution layers concentrated in the middle of the stack.

**What's new**
- RL gains are concentrated in a small subset of layers, often just one.
- High-contribution layers consistently appear in the middle of the transformer.
- Layer rankings are stable across models, tasks, and RL algorithms.
- Introduces 'layer contribution' metric to quantify isolated layer improvement.

### [AGC-Bench: Measuring Artificial General Creativity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01152v1)
**Authors:** Roger Beaty, Vijeta Deshpande, Clin K. Y. Lai, Anna Attuch, Namrata Shivagunde, Swastik Roy, Rajkumar Pujari, Paul V. DiStefano, Sherin Muckatira, Claire E. Stevenson, Mikhail Gronas, Anna Rumshisky
**Published:** 2026-07-01
**Summary:** AGC-Bench is a unified benchmark for measuring artificial general creativity in LLMs, revealing a single creativity factor 'c' and showing humans still lead.

**What's new**
- Introduces AGC-Bench, a benchmark of 78 datasets across six creativity domains from a systematic review of 3,101 papers.
- Applies Judge Response Theory to calibrate LLM-as-judge bias, producing AGC-Judge, an open-weight model for scoring creativity.
- Factor analysis across 83 LLMs reveals a single creativity factor 'c' explaining 81.5% of variance, separable from general intelligence.
- Prompting models to 'be creative' boosts performance more than enabling reasoning, showing the benchmark tracks creativity over general ability.
- On a human-matched subset, the top human still outperforms the top LLM on creativity.

**Summary:** Flat bands near the Fermi level observed in bulk type-II Weyl semimetal TaRhTe4, unpredicted by theory.

**What's new**
